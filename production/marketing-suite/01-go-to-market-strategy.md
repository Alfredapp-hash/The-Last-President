# 01 — Go-To-Market Strategy

## Objective

Launch and scale **Baren Sump and The Last President** as a premium literary-thriller trilogy with a strong anti-formula identity:

- dark political thriller tone
- high-concept hooks
- child-centered ethical framing
- "case file" positioning over chosen-one fantasy

## Market position

### What this series is

- Literary speculative political thriller
- Institutional dread + moral architecture + voice-first storytelling
- Trilogy with a completed narrative arc

### What this series is not

- YA chosen-one fantasy
- Pure procedural with low emotional stakes
- Generic dystopian action franchise

## Audience segments

1. **Primary: Literary-thriller readers**
   - follow political/technological thrillers
   - attracted to strong prose + ideas
2. **Secondary: Speculative fiction readers**
   - enjoy near-future social systems
   - respond to conceptual hooks and dark aesthetics
3. **Tertiary: Advocacy/intellectual communities**
   - interested in ethics of institutions, surveillance, child-protection themes

## Funnel model

1. **Top of funnel**
   - social hooks, quote cards, short videos, press features
2. **Mid funnel**
   - email list growth, sample chapter offer, author/series story
3. **Bottom funnel**
   - retailer pages, pre-order windows, launch week discounts/bundles
4. **Retention**
   - sequel laddering (Book 1 -> Book 2 -> Book 3), reading-group prompts, newsletter arcs

## Offer architecture

- Core offer: single-title purchase by entry point
- Ladder offer: trilogy progression messaging
- Premium offer: complete trilogy bundle language + limited signed run (optional)

## Messaging hierarchy

1. Hook sentence (one-line concept)
2. Ethical differentiator (children are not evidence)
3. Stakes + atmosphere
4. Call to action (read sample / buy / join ledger)

## Channel priorities

1. **Landing site + email**
2. **Short-form social + quote cards**
3. **Retailer optimization**
4. **PR + reviewer outreach**
5. **Paid acquisition experiments**

## Success criteria (first 90 days)

- Positive conversion trend on site and retailer pages
- Consistent weekly audience growth
- Review base established for each book
- CPI/CPL targets validated for paid channels
- Repeat purchase flow from Book 1 readers into Book 2/3
