## Short blurbs (~75 words each)

### Book One: The Last President
When Nikola Veyra dies in an unnatural storm, he leaves a prediction card scorched with impossible names—including Baren Sump and THE LAST PRESIDENT. What follows is a case file, not a fable: algorithms that preach, families that buy tomorrow, and a child who learns that being watched is not the same as being protected.

### Book Two: Children of Tomorrow
Protection ends. Accountability begins. Baren learns about a grounded plane from a cereal box. The Look-Away Ledger and the RAF-One archive turn one child's story into a system story. Courts without windows. Adults who were almost brave. The controlling sentence is unbreakable: **children are not evidence.**

### Book Three: The Black Path
The black path is not a tunnel—it is a refusal. Baren will not remain available as proof, witness, remedy, or adult repair tool. Book Three compresses toward a quiet ending: ordinary remains enough. No crowned climax. No child-saving-the-world turn.

## Amazon / retailer description (series)

**BAREN SUMP AND THE LAST PRESIDENT — Complete Trilogy**

Dark political thrillers about power, prophecy, and the future that's already here.

This trilogy (~232,000 words, 84 chapters) follows Baren Sump from scorched prediction card to grounded aircraft—from spectacle to protection to refusal. It is literary speculative fiction for readers who want institutional dread, moral architecture, and a child-centered story that never treats its protagonist as a chosen one.

**Book One — The Last President:** Prophecy as infrastructure. The confession machine. The family that bought tomorrow.

**Book Two — Children of Tomorrow:** The Look-Away Ledger. Who looked away. Children are not evidence.

**Book Three — The Black Path:** Consent, availability, and the quiet victory of ordinary life.

*For readers of near-future systems fiction, procedural thrillers, and literary voice-driven speculative work.*
